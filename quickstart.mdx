---
title: "Quickstart"
description: "Create your first Heirloom vault and set up inheritance in under 10 minutes."
---

This guide walks you through the complete vault owner setup: installing a wallet, funding it with testnet assets, creating a vault, and sending your first heartbeat. At the end, you will know what heirs need to do when the time comes.

<Note>
  All steps in this guide use the Stacks testnet. No real Bitcoin or real sBTC is at risk. The testnet contract is deployed at `STZJWVYSKRYV1XBGS8BZ4F81E32RHBREQSE5WAJM.heirloom-vault-v10`.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install a Stacks wallet">
    Heirloom supports Leather and Xverse. Install one before opening the app.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Leather">
        1. Go to [leather.io](https://leather.io) and install the browser extension for Chrome or Firefox.
        2. Open the extension and choose **Create new wallet**.
        3. Write down your 24-word secret key and store it somewhere safe — not in a screenshot, not in a cloud note.
        4. Set a password to unlock the extension locally.
        5. Switch to **Testnet**: open the extension menu (top-right), go to **Settings → Network**, and select **Testnet**.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Xverse">
        1. Go to [xverse.app](https://xverse.app) and install the browser extension or mobile app.
        2. Open Xverse and choose **Create wallet**.
        3. Back up your seed phrase securely before continuing.
        4. Switch to testnet: open **Settings → Network** and select **Testnet**.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    <Warning>
      Always back up your seed phrase before using any wallet with real assets. Heirloom cannot recover lost wallets.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get testnet STX, sBTC, and USDCx">
    You need testnet STX to pay transaction fees, plus sBTC or USDCx to deposit into the vault.

    **Testnet STX faucet**

    Visit the [Stacks testnet faucet](https://explorer.hiro.so/sandbox/faucet?chain=testnet) and request STX to your wallet address. Funds usually arrive within 30 seconds.

    **Testnet sBTC and USDCx**

    The Heirloom app includes a testnet faucet on the **Faucet** page. Connect your wallet (step 4 below), then:

    1. Navigate to **Faucet** in the app sidebar.
    2. Click **Mint test sBTC** to receive tokens from `ST1F7QA2MDF17S807EPA36TSS8AMEFY4KA9TVGWXT.sbtc-token`.
    3. Click **Mint test USDCx** to receive tokens from `ST1PQHQKV0RJXZFY1DGX8MNSNYVE3VGZJSRTPGZGM.usdcx`.

    <Note>
      These are testnet-only tokens with no real value. They exist solely for testing vault creation and claiming flows.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Heirloom app">
    Make sure the frontend development server is running, then open your browser to:

    ```text
    http://localhost:5173
    ```

    You will see the Heirloom landing page with a **Connect wallet** button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your wallet">
    1. Click **Connect wallet** in the top-right corner.
    2. Select **Leather** or **Xverse** from the dialog.
    3. Approve the connection request in the wallet popup.

    Your Stacks address appears in the header once connected. Verify it matches the address you funded in step 2.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to Create vault">
    Click **Create vault** in the sidebar, or use the button on the Dashboard home screen. This opens the vault creation form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure heartbeat interval and grace period">
    Set the two timing parameters that control when your vault becomes claimable.

    | Parameter | What it controls | Example value |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Heartbeat interval** | How often you must send a transaction to keep the vault active | 30 days |
    | **Grace period** | How long heirs wait after a missed heartbeat before they can claim | 7 days |

    Enter `30` for the heartbeat interval and `7` for the grace period. Both fields accept values from 1 to 365.

    <Tip>
      For testing, use short intervals like 1–3 days so you can simulate a missed heartbeat and observe the grace and claimable states without waiting weeks.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add heirs">
    Click **Add heir** to add each beneficiary. For each heir you need:

    - **Stacks address** — the address that will receive the funds
    - **Percentage split** — entered as a percentage (the app converts to basis points internally)

    Example configuration with two heirs:

    | Heir | Address | Split |
    |---|---|---|
    | Heir 1 | `ST2CY5V39NHDPWSXMW9QDT3HC3GD6Q6XX4CFRK9AG` | 70% |
    | Heir 2 | `ST2JHG361ZXG51QTKY2NQCVBPPRRE2KZB1HR05NNS` | 30% |

    All splits must sum to exactly 100%. You can add up to 10 heirs.

    <Warning>
      Double-check every heir address before confirming. Stacks addresses are case-sensitive and the contract cannot reassign funds after vault creation.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Optionally add a guardian">
    A guardian is an address that can pause the grace period once for 30 days. This is useful if you want a trusted person — a family member, attorney, or co-signer — to have a brief window to intervene if the vault enters grace unexpectedly while you are still alive.

    To add a guardian, enter a Stacks address in the **Guardian address** field. Leave it blank if you do not want one.

    <Note>
      The guardian can only pause the grace period once, for exactly 30 days. They cannot withdraw funds, change heirs, or reset the vault.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deposit sBTC and/or USDCx">
    Enter the amounts you want to lock in the vault:

    - **sBTC amount** — denominated in sBTC (e.g., `0.001`)
    - **USDCx amount** — denominated in USDCx (e.g., `100`)

    You can deposit one or both tokens. The amounts must not exceed your wallet balance.

    <Tip>
      Use small amounts for your first test vault. You can always emergency-withdraw before distribution if you need to retrieve funds during testing.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and confirm">
    The final step shows a summary of your vault configuration:

    - Heartbeat interval and grace period
    - Heir list with addresses and percentage splits
    - Guardian address (if set)
    - Token deposit amounts

    Review every field, then click **Create vault**. Your wallet will prompt you to sign two transactions:

    1. **Token approvals** — authorizing the contract to transfer your sBTC and/or USDCx
    2. **Vault creation** — calling the contract's `create-vault` function

    Approve both. On-chain confirmation typically takes 10–30 seconds on testnet. Once confirmed, you are redirected to the Dashboard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send your first heartbeat">
    After the vault is created, go to the **Dashboard** in the sidebar. You will see your vault listed with status **active** and a countdown to the next required heartbeat.

    Click **Send heartbeat** to submit your first heartbeat transaction. This resets the countdown timer and confirms to the contract that you are active.

    ```text
    Vault status:  active
    Last heartbeat: just now
    Next heartbeat due: in 30 days
    ```

    <Note>
      The Dashboard polls the contract state every 30 seconds. If the status does not update immediately after confirmation, wait a moment and refresh.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="What heirs do when the time comes">
    When the vault becomes claimable (heartbeat missed, grace period elapsed), heirs follow these steps:

    1. Open the Heirloom app at `http://localhost:5173`.
    2. Connect their Stacks wallet (Leather or Xverse).
    3. Navigate to **Claim** in the sidebar.
    4. Find the vault by the owner's address — it will show status **claimable**.
    5. Click **Claim sBTC** and/or **Claim USDCx** to submit their claim transactions.

    The contract calculates each heir's share from the basis-point splits configured at vault creation and transfers the funds directly to the heir's wallet address. No one else can claim on their behalf.
  </Step>
</Steps>
