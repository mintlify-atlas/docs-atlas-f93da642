---
title: "Guardian system"
description: "How an optional trusted guardian can pause the inheritance countdown during the grace period."
---

A guardian is an optional trusted Stacks address you can designate when creating your vault. The guardian has one narrow power: they can extend your vault's deadline by 30 days if you miss a heartbeat and the vault enters the grace period.

## What a guardian can do

During the grace period, the guardian can call `guardian-pause(vault-owner)` to add 30 days to the effective deadline.

```clarity GUARDIAN-PAUSE-BONUS constant
;; 30 days in seconds
(define-constant GUARDIAN-PAUSE-BONUS u2592000)
```

When the pause is applied:

- The `guardian-pause-used` flag is set to `true` on the vault
- The effective deadline becomes: `heartbeat-interval + grace-period + 2,592,000 seconds`
- All state calculations immediately reflect the extended deadline
- The vault may move back from `claimable` to `grace` if the extension pushes the deadline past the current elapsed time

## What a guardian cannot do

The guardian role is intentionally limited. A guardian **cannot**:

- Withdraw any assets from the vault
- Claim assets on behalf of heirs
- Send a heartbeat (only the vault owner can)
- Modify the heir list or splits
- Change the vault configuration
- Pause the vault more than once

<Note>
The guardian's only action is `guardian-pause()`. They have no access to your funds and cannot redirect inheritance to themselves.
</Note>

## Restrictions on the guardian pause

| Restriction | Detail |
|---|---|
| Timing | Only callable during grace period (`elapsed >= heartbeat-interval` AND `elapsed < deadline`) |
| Frequency | Once per vault lifetime (`guardian-pause-used` flag prevents reuse) |
| Who can call it | Only the designated guardian address |
| Effect duration | Permanent for that vault — the 30-day bonus is added to the deadline and does not reset |

<Warning>
The guardian pause can only be used **once**. If the vault re-enters grace after a recovered heartbeat, the pause cannot be used a second time. Plan accordingly when choosing your guardian.
</Warning>

## Choosing a guardian

Your guardian should be:

- **Trusted**: they will know your vault is overdue, which signals your status to them
- **Reachable**: they need to be able to respond quickly during the grace period
- **Technically capable**: they must be able to send a Stacks transaction from their own wallet
- **Not an heir**: while not technically prohibited, having a guardian who is also an heir creates a conflict of interest — they could choose not to pause in order to claim sooner

<Tip>
A good guardian is someone like a close family member, attorney, or financial advisor who is not named as an heir and who you trust to act in your interest.
</Tip>

## Setting a guardian

The guardian is an optional field in the vault creation wizard. You can set it when you first create your vault.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the vault creation wizard">
    From the Dashboard, click **Create Vault** to open the setup flow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the guardian address">
    In the Guardian step, enter the Stacks address (`SP...`) of your chosen guardian. This field is optional — leave it blank if you do not want a guardian.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the configuration">
    Review the guardian address before submitting. The guardian is set at vault creation and cannot be changed after the vault is deployed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
The guardian address is **set at creation and cannot be updated** after the vault is deployed. Choose carefully, and verify the address before confirming.
</Warning>

## Viewing guardian status

The vault detail page on the Dashboard shows:

- The guardian address (if set)
- Whether the guardian pause has been used (`guardian-pause-used` flag)
- The extended deadline (if the pause has been applied)

If you are a guardian, you can look up the vault owner's address in the Dashboard to see the vault state and determine whether a pause is needed.
