---
title: "Heirs and splits"
description: "How to designate beneficiaries and allocate inheritance shares."
---

Heirs are the Stacks addresses that receive your vault's assets when the vault becomes claimable. You can designate up to 10 heirs per vault, each with a defined percentage of the total balance.

## Splits and basis points

Shares are expressed in **basis points**, where `10000` equals 100%. This gives you two decimal places of precision without floating-point arithmetic in the contract.

| Basis points | Percentage |
|---|---|
| 10000 | 100% |
| 5000 | 50% |
| 2500 | 25% |
| 1000 | 10% |
| 500 | 5% |
| 100 | 1% |

```clarity BASIS-POINTS constant
(define-constant BASIS-POINTS u10000)
```

<Warning>
The splits across all heirs **must sum to exactly 10000**. The contract enforces this at creation and on every update. If your splits do not add up to 10000, the transaction will be rejected.
</Warning>

## Example allocations

| Heir | Role | Basis points | Percentage |
|------|------|---|---|
| `SP1ABC...` | Spouse | 6000 | 60% |
| `SP2DEF...` | Child 1 | 2500 | 25% |
| `SP3GHI...` | Child 2 | 1500 | 15% |
| **Total** | | **10000** | **100%** |

## What heirs receive

Each heir receives a proportional share of every asset in the vault at the time of their claim. Heirloom vaults hold two asset types:

- **sBTC** — wrapped Bitcoin on Stacks
- **USDCx** — cross-chain USDC on Stacks

The share for each asset is calculated identically:

```
sbtc-share  = (sbtc-balance  * split-bps) / 10000
usdcx-share = (usdcx-balance * split-bps) / 10000
```

This calculation happens at claim time using the vault's actual balance, so late deposits before a claim are included in the distribution.

## The claim process

Each heir claims independently. There is no coordination required between heirs, and one heir claiming does not affect another's ability to claim.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Vault becomes claimable">
    The vault's full deadline passes (heartbeat interval + grace period + any guardian extension). The vault state moves to `claimable`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Heir calls claim">
    The heir sends a `claim(vault-owner)` transaction from their own wallet. The contract verifies:
    - The vault is in claimable state
    - The caller's address is in the heir list
    - The heir has not already claimed
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assets transferred">
    The contract transfers the heir's calculated share of sBTC and USDCx directly to their address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Last claim triggers distribution">
    When the final heir claims their share, the contract sets `is-distributed = true` and the vault moves to the `distributed` state permanently.
  </Step>
</Steps>


## Updating heirs

While your vault is in **active** state, you can call `update-heirs()` to change the heir list and splits. The same rules apply:

- Up to 10 heirs
- Splits must sum to exactly 10000
- Changes take effect immediately for future claims

<Note>
You cannot update heirs once the vault is in grace or claimable state. Update your heirs while the vault is active.
</Note>

## Heir discovery in the app

The Heirloom Dashboard helps heirs find vaults that name them as beneficiaries:

- **Auto-scan**: the app queries the Hiro API to find all vaults where your connected address appears as an heir. This runs automatically when you open the Dashboard.
- **Manual lookup**: heirs can enter a vault owner's address directly to look up a specific vault, without needing the owner to share a link.

<Tip>
Tell your heirs about Heirloom and make sure they have their Stacks wallet set up before an emergency. The claim process requires them to send a transaction, which means having a funded wallet and knowing how to use it.
</Tip>

## Limits and constraints

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum heirs per vault | 10 |
| Minimum split per heir | 1 basis point (0.01%) |
| Total splits required | Exactly 10000 (100%) |
| Supported assets | sBTC, USDCx |
| Claim window | After claimable state; no expiry |
