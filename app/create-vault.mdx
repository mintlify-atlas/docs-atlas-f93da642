---
title: "Create a vault"
description: "Step-by-step guide to setting up your Heirloom inheritance vault."
---

The vault creation wizard walks you through four steps: configuring timing parameters, adding heirs, making an optional initial deposit, and confirming the transaction on-chain.

Navigate to [http://localhost:5173/create](http://localhost:5173/create) or click **Create Vault** from the landing page. You must have a wallet connected before proceeding.

## Wizard walkthrough

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 0 — Parameters">
    Set the two timing parameters that control your vault's heartbeat cycle.

    | Parameter | Description | Recommended |
    |-----------|-------------|-------------|
    | **Heartbeat interval** | How often you must send a proof-of-life signal (1–365 days) | 30 days |
    | **Grace period** | Additional time heirs must wait after a missed heartbeat before claiming (1–365 days) | 7 days |

    **How to choose:**

    - The **heartbeat interval** determines how frequently you must log in and send a heartbeat. A 30-day interval is a practical default — long enough that it's not burdensome, short enough to catch issues quickly.
    - The **grace period** gives you a buffer if you miss a heartbeat (travel, illness, connectivity issues). A 7-day grace period provides a safety window while keeping the vault responsive.
    - Shorter intervals and grace periods mean faster inheritance resolution but require more active monitoring.
    - Longer intervals reduce maintenance burden but delay heirs longer in an emergency.

    <Tip>
      Set a calendar reminder for a few days before your heartbeat interval expires. For a 30-day interval, schedule a monthly reminder on day 25.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 1 — Heirs">
    Add the Stacks addresses of your beneficiaries and specify how assets should be split.

    **Adding heirs:**

    1. Enter each heir's Stacks address in `SP...` format (Stacks principal).
    2. Assign a whole-number percentage to each heir.
    3. All percentages must sum to exactly **100%**.
    4. You can add up to **10 heirs**.

    **Percentage allocation example:**

    | Heir address | Allocation |
    |---|---|
    | `SP1ABC...` | 60% |
    | `SP2DEF...` | 40% |

    **Optional guardian:**

    You may designate a guardian address. The guardian can pause the vault once — useful if you are temporarily incapacitated and need to prevent a premature claim while you recover. The guardian does not receive any assets.

    <Warning>
      Double-check every heir address before proceeding. Stacks addresses are case-sensitive. If you enter an incorrect address, you will need to call `updateHeirs` after vault creation to correct it — the vault cannot be edited during the wizard.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2 — Deposit">
    Fund your vault with sBTC, USDCx, or both. This step is optional at creation time — you can deposit from the Dashboard later.

    | Token | Description |
    |-------|-------------|
    | **sBTC** | Synthetic Bitcoin on Stacks. Value fluctuates with BTC price. |
    | **USDCx** | Dollar-stable token on Stacks. Value pegged to USD. |

    Enter the amounts you want to lock into the vault. Both fields accept decimal values.

    **Depositing later:**

    If you prefer to review the vault first, click **Skip** or leave both fields at zero. The vault is created without funds and you can add sBTC and USDCx from the Dashboard at any time.

    <Note>
      USDCx is useful for providing heirs with a predictable dollar-denominated portion of the inheritance, separate from the more volatile sBTC component.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3 — Review and confirm">
    The final step displays a full summary before submitting anything on-chain.

    **Review the summary:**

    - Heartbeat interval and grace period
    - List of heirs with their percentage allocations
    - Guardian address (if set)
    - Deposit amounts for sBTC and USDCx

    If anything looks wrong, use the **Back** button to return to the relevant step.

    **Confirming:**

    1. Click **Confirm and Create Vault**.
    2. Your wallet opens a signing dialog — review and approve the `create-vault` transaction.
    3. The wizard polls for on-chain confirmation every 5 seconds and displays a progress indicator.
    4. Once the vault is confirmed, any deposit transactions are submitted automatically (one per token).
    5. After all transactions confirm, you are redirected to the **Dashboard**.

    <Note>
      The create-vault transaction and deposit transactions are separate on-chain calls. If a deposit transaction fails after the vault is created, you can re-deposit from the Dashboard — the vault itself remains intact.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## After creation

Once redirected to the Dashboard, your vault is in **Active** state. The countdown timer starts from your chosen heartbeat interval. Send your first heartbeat before the timer reaches zero to keep the vault active.

See [Dashboard](/app/dashboard) for a full explanation of vault states and the heartbeat workflow.

<Note>
  After a vault has fully distributed (all heirs have claimed), you can create a new vault from the same Stacks address. The previous vault is closed on-chain and does not interfere with the new one.
</Note>
