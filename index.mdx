## Bitcoin inheritance, solved on-chain

Heirloom is a trustless inheritance protocol built on Stacks. Lock sBTC and USDCx into a Clarity smart contract, designate your heirs with percentage splits, and maintain a periodic heartbeat from your wallet. If your heartbeat stops, your designated beneficiaries can claim their shares — automatically, without lawyers, custodians, or seed phrase sharing.

## Why Heirloom?

An estimated 3–4 million Bitcoin are permanently lost. A growing portion is due to holder death or incapacitation with no succession plan. Every existing solution requires a compromise:

| Approach | Problem |
|---|---|
| Seed phrase in a safe | Exposes full wallet access to a third party |
| Multisig with family | Requires technical sophistication from every keyholder |
| Centralized custody | Defeats the purpose of self-custody |
| Estate lawyers | Slow, expensive, and cannot interact with smart contracts |
| Do nothing | The most common approach — Bitcoin is permanently lost |

Heirloom resolves this tension: **custody stays in the owner's hands** while inheritance becomes automatic and trustless.

## How the vault lifecycle works

```
CREATED → ACTIVE → GRACE → CLAIMABLE → DISTRIBUTED
              ↑         ↑          ↑
              └─────────┴──────────┘
                   heartbeat() resets countdown
```

The vault state is **computed on every read** from `stacks-block-time` and `last-heartbeat` — no stale state, no manual state-update transactions required.
